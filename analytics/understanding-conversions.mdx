# Understanding Conversions

A conversion is any action a visitor takes on your website that matters to your business. It's the moment someone goes from "just browsing" to "actually interested" -- and tracking these moments is one of the most important things your website analytics can do.

If you only look at one set of numbers, make it your conversions. Everything else is context. Conversions are the bottom line.

## What Counts as a Conversion?

For wellness businesses like yours, conversions usually fall into a few categories. During your website build, we set up tracking for the actions that matter most to your specific business.

### Common Conversions for Wellness Businesses

- **Booking a discovery call** -- someone fills out your booking form or clicks through to your Calendly/booking page
- **Filling out a contact form** -- a visitor sends you a message through your website's contact form
- **Signing up for a newsletter** -- someone joins your email list
- **Downloading a resource** -- a visitor downloads your guide, ebook, or freebie
- **Clicking a phone number** -- someone taps your phone number to call you (especially on mobile)
- **Clicking an email address** -- someone clicks your email link to reach out directly
- **Starting a checkout** -- for retreats or products, when someone begins the purchase process
- **Completing a purchase** -- the ultimate conversion for e-commerce or retreat bookings

We tailor the conversion tracking to your business during the build. If you want to track additional actions, just let us know on Slack and we can add them.

## How to See Your Conversions

Depending on your plan, you can find your conversion data in a few places.

### Monthly Report (Care Plan Clients)

Your [monthly performance report](/hosting-care-plan/monthly-reports) includes a conversions section. We highlight the total number of conversions, the conversion rate, and any notable trends. This is the easiest way to stay on top of your numbers.

### PostHog (All Clients)

PostHog tracks every form submission, button click, and key interaction on your site. If you have dashboard access, you can see conversion events in real time.

### GA4 (Growth/Signature Clients)

In Google Analytics, navigate to Reports -> Engagement -> Conversions. This shows you which conversion events fired and how often. You can also see which traffic sources drive the most conversions, which is incredibly useful for understanding your marketing ROI.

## Understanding Conversion Rate

Your conversion rate is the percentage of visitors who take a desired action. It's calculated with a simple formula:

**Conversion Rate = (Number of Conversions / Number of Visitors) x 100**

So if you had 1,000 visitors and 30 of them filled out your contact form, your conversion rate is 3%.

### What's a Good Conversion Rate?

This depends on your industry, your traffic sources, and what you're asking people to do. Here are some general benchmarks for service-based wellness businesses.

| Action | Typical Range | Doing Well |
|---|---|---|
| Contact form submission | 2-5% | 5%+ |
| Newsletter signup | 1-3% | 3%+ |
| Discovery call booking | 1-3% | 3%+ |
| Resource download | 5-15% | 15%+ |
| Phone number click | 1-2% | 2%+ |

<Callout kind="info">
These benchmarks are rough guidelines, not rules. A retreat center with a niche audience and targeted traffic might see much higher conversion rates than a general wellness coach with broad traffic. Context matters more than benchmarks.
</Callout>

### Why Conversion Rate Matters More Than Traffic

Here's a scenario that illustrates why conversions matter more than raw traffic.

**Website A:** 5,000 visitors, 1% conversion rate = 50 leads per month
**Website B:** 1,000 visitors, 5% conversion rate = 50 leads per month

Both websites generate the same number of leads. But Website B is doing it with one-fifth of the traffic. If you could improve Website A's conversion rate from 1% to 2%, you'd double your leads without needing a single extra visitor.

<Callout kind="tip">
Even small improvements in conversion rate can make a big difference. Going from 2% to 3% means 50% more leads from the same traffic. That's worth paying attention to.
</Callout>

## Tips to Improve Your Conversions

You don't need to overhaul your entire website to see better conversion rates. Often, small changes make a meaningful difference. Here are practical things you can do.

### 1. Make Your Call-to-Action Buttons Clear and Visible

Your CTA buttons should be obvious and compelling. "Book a Free Discovery Call" is better than "Learn More." "Get Your Free Retreat Guide" is better than "Submit."

**Tips for better CTAs:**
- Use action-oriented language ("Book," "Download," "Get Started")
- Make buttons visually distinct from the rest of the page (contrasting color, enough size)
- Place CTAs where people can see them without scrolling too far
- Repeat your main CTA multiple times on long pages

### 2. Reduce the Number of Form Fields

Every field you add to a form reduces the number of people who complete it. Only ask for what you truly need.

**For a contact form:** Name and email are usually enough. Add phone number only if you really need it. Skip everything else.

**For a booking form:** Name, email, and a brief message or date preference. You can collect the rest during the actual conversation.

**Rule of thumb:** If you can get the information later (in an email or during a call), don't ask for it in the form.

### 3. Add Social Proof Near Your CTAs

People are more likely to take action when they see that others have done the same. Place testimonials, reviews, or trust signals near your conversion points.

**Effective social proof:**
- A short testimonial quote right above or below your CTA button
- Star ratings or review counts
- "Trusted by X clients" or "X retreats hosted"
- Logos of publications you've been featured in
- Before-and-after stories from your clients

### 4. Make Sure Your Site Loads Fast on Mobile

More than half of your visitors are probably on their phones. If your site takes more than 3 seconds to load on mobile, a significant number of people will leave before it even finishes loading.

We optimize your site for speed during the build, but there are things that can slow it down over time (like very large images). If you suspect speed is an issue, let us know and we'll run a performance check.

### 5. Reduce Friction in the Conversion Path

Think about the journey from landing on your site to taking action. Every extra step or distraction is friction that costs you conversions.

- Don't make people hunt for your contact form. Link to it prominently.
- If you're sending people to an external booking tool, make sure the link opens smoothly.
- Remove unnecessary navigation options on landing pages that might distract from the main action.
- Make sure your CTA is above the fold (visible without scrolling) on your most important pages.

### 6. Match Your Message to Your Traffic Source

If someone clicks an Instagram post about your breathwork retreat, they should land on your breathwork retreat page -- not your generic homepage. The more relevant the landing page is to what brought someone there, the higher your conversion rate.

**This is especially important for paid advertising.** If you're running Google Ads or Meta Ads, make sure your ad leads to a specific, relevant page. We can help you set up dedicated landing pages if needed.

### 7. Test One Thing at a Time

If you want to improve your conversion rate, change one element at a time and see what happens. Change the headline. Wait a month. Check your numbers. Then change the button text. Wait. Check.

Changing everything at once makes it impossible to know what actually made the difference.

## Conversion Tracking Across Platforms

If you have both PostHog and GA4, you might notice slightly different conversion numbers between the two. This is normal and happens because:

- **GA4 only counts visitors who accept cookies.** If someone declines cookies and then fills out your form, PostHog captures it but GA4 doesn't.
- **Different attribution models.** GA4 and PostHog attribute conversions to traffic sources differently.
- **Timing differences.** The platforms may process events with slight delays.

**Our recommendation:** Use PostHog for the total picture and GA4 for understanding which marketing channels drive conversions. Together, they give you a complete view.
