# Analytics Overview

Understanding how people find and use your website is one of the most valuable things you can do for your business. Analytics give you the answers to questions like "Is my website actually working?" and "Where are my visitors coming from?"

We've set up analytics for your website in a way that respects your visitors' privacy while still giving you the data you need to make smart decisions.

## Our Analytics Approach

We use **PostHog** as our primary analytics platform. If you haven't heard of it, that's okay -- most people haven't. But it's exactly the right tool for wellness businesses that care about privacy and simplicity.

For clients on our Growth or Signature plans, we also set up **Google Analytics 4 (GA4)** and **Meta Pixel** for deeper marketing insights and ad tracking.

Here's why we made these choices and what they mean for you.

## PostHog: Your Privacy-First Analytics

PostHog is our go-to analytics tool for every website we build. Here's what makes it special.

### EU-Hosted and Cookieless

PostHog stores all data on servers in the European Union. It doesn't use cookies to track visitors. This means two things that matter a lot:

1. **No cookie banner needed.** Your visitors won't see an annoying "Accept cookies?" popup when they land on your site. That's a better first impression and means you see data for all visitors, not just the ones who click "Accept."

2. **GDPR-friendly by default.** Because no personal data is stored and no cookies are used, PostHog is compliant with European privacy regulations out of the box. You don't need a privacy policy that's 10 pages long.

### What PostHog Tracks

Even without cookies, PostHog gives you a clear picture of how your website is performing:

- **Page views** -- which pages people visit and how often
- **Button clicks** -- what people click on (CTAs, navigation links, etc.)
- **Form submissions** -- when someone fills out a contact form or booking request
- **Scroll depth** -- how far down the page people actually read
- **Session replays** -- anonymized recordings of how visitors navigate your site (available on request)
- **User paths** -- how people move through your site from page to page

<Callout kind="info">
Cookieless analytics means your visitor counts might look different from what you'd see in Google Analytics. That's normal and expected. PostHog counts sessions differently because it can't use cookies to identify returning visitors across sessions. The data is still accurate and useful -- it's just measured differently.
</Callout>

### Why Not Just Use Google Analytics?

We get this question a lot. Google Analytics is a great tool, but it comes with trade-offs:

- It requires a cookie consent banner (which many visitors dismiss or decline)
- It sends data to Google's servers in the US (which raises GDPR questions)
- Visitors who decline cookies are invisible in GA4, which can skew your data
- The interface can be overwhelming if you're not a data analyst

PostHog avoids all of these issues while still giving you the metrics that actually matter for your business.

That said, if you're running paid ads on Google or Meta, you need GA4 and the Meta Pixel for proper tracking and optimization. That's why we include them for our Growth and Signature clients.

## What Gets Tracked on Your Website

Here's a breakdown of the data we collect and how it helps you.

### Visits and Page Views

The basics: how many people visit your site and which pages they look at. This tells you whether your marketing efforts are driving traffic and which content resonates most with your audience.

### Button Clicks and Interactions

We track when visitors click on your call-to-action buttons, navigation links, and other interactive elements. This shows you what's working and what's being ignored.

**Why this matters:** If you have a "Book a Call" button that nobody clicks, we know the placement, wording, or design might need improvement.

### Form Submissions

Every form submission is tracked as a conversion. Whether it's a contact form, newsletter signup, or retreat booking request, you'll know exactly how many people are taking action.

### Scroll Depth

Not everyone reads your entire page. Scroll depth tracking shows you how far down visitors get before they leave. This is incredibly useful for long pages.

**Why this matters:** If you have an important call-to-action at the bottom of a page but most people only scroll 60% of the way down, they never see it. We might recommend moving it higher.

## Making Sense of the Numbers

Analytics can feel overwhelming if you're not used to looking at data. Here are a few principles to keep in mind.

### Focus on Trends, Not Absolutes

Your traffic went from 347 visitors to 312 this month. Is that bad? Not necessarily. Monthly fluctuations are normal. What matters is the trend over 3-6 months. Are things generally going up, down, or staying flat?

### Quality Over Quantity

100 visitors who are genuinely interested in your retreats are worth more than 10,000 random visitors who bounce immediately. Don't chase big numbers for their own sake. Focus on attracting the right people and converting them.

### Tie Data to Actions

The best analytics insight is one that leads to a specific action. "Our retreat page has high traffic but low conversions" leads to "Let's improve the call-to-action on that page." Data without action is just entertainment.
