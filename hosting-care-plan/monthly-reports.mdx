# Monthly Performance Reports

If you're on the Care Plan, you receive a monthly performance report delivered straight to your Slack channel. It lands around the first week of each month and covers the previous month's data.

The goal isn't to overwhelm you with numbers. It's to give you a clear picture of how your website is performing and where there might be opportunities to improve.

## When and How You'll Receive It

Your report arrives via Slack, usually in the first week of the month. It covers the full previous calendar month. So a report delivered in early February covers all of January.

We send it as a well-formatted message with the key numbers highlighted, along with a brief commentary on what we're seeing and any recommendations we have. If you have questions about anything in the report, just reply in the thread and we'll break it down further.

## Key Metrics Explained

Here's what each metric in your report means, in plain language.

### Visitors (Unique Users)

This is the number of individual people who visited your website during the month. If the same person visits three times, they count as one visitor.

This is your most important top-level number. It tells you how many people are actually seeing your website. Think of it as foot traffic for your online presence.

### Page Views

Page views count the total number of pages viewed across all visitors. This number is always higher than your visitor count because most people browse more than one page when they visit.

If you have 500 visitors and 1,500 page views, that means each person viewed about 3 pages on average. That's a good sign -- it means people are exploring your site, not just bouncing off the first page.

### Top Pages

This shows which pages on your site get the most traffic. Your homepage will almost always be at the top, but pay attention to what comes next.

**Why this matters:** If your retreat page or services page consistently ranks high, that tells you people are actively interested in what you offer. If an important page barely gets any views, it might need better visibility in your navigation or more internal links pointing to it.

### Traffic Sources

Where are your visitors coming from? This breaks down into a few categories:

- **Organic Search** -- people who found you through Google or another search engine
- **Direct** -- people who typed your URL directly or used a bookmark
- **Social** -- visitors from Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, or other social platforms
- **Referral** -- visitors who clicked a link to your site from another website
- **Paid** -- traffic from any paid advertising campaigns you're running

**Why this matters:** This tells you which of your marketing efforts are actually driving people to your website. If you're posting on Instagram every day but most traffic comes from Google, that's useful information for deciding where to focus your energy.

### Average Session Duration

This is how long people spend on your site, on average. A "session" starts when someone arrives and ends when they leave or are inactive for a while.

For service-based wellness businesses, anything above 1-2 minutes is solid. If people are spending 3+ minutes, they're genuinely engaging with your content. Under 30 seconds usually means people aren't finding what they expected.

### Bounce Rate

The bounce rate is the percentage of visitors who leave after viewing only one page. They landed on your site and left without clicking anything else.

**Lower is better.** A bounce rate between 30-50% is typical for service businesses. Above 70% might mean your landing page isn't encouraging people to explore further.

<Callout kind="info">
A high bounce rate on your contact page isn't necessarily bad. If someone lands on your contact page and calls you directly, that looks like a "bounce" in analytics even though it's actually a win.
</Callout>

### Conversions

Conversions track the actions that matter most to your business. Depending on what we set up during your website build, this might include:

- Booking a discovery call
- Filling out a contact form
- Signing up for your newsletter
- Downloading a guide or resource
- Clicking your phone number or email address

We'll highlight how many conversions happened that month and, where possible, show the conversion rate (what percentage of visitors took action).

## How to Read Your Report

The numbers themselves are useful, but the real value is in the story they tell together. Here's how to think about it.

### Look for Trends, Not Snapshots

A single month's numbers don't tell you much on their own. What matters is the direction things are moving over time. Is traffic going up? Are more people converting? Is your bounce rate improving?

We include month-over-month comparisons in your report so you can spot these trends easily.

<Callout kind="tip">
Don't stress about small monthly fluctuations. Seasonal patterns, holidays, and even the weather can affect your website traffic. Look at 3-month trends instead of obsessing over any single month.
</Callout>

### Connect the Dots

Your report becomes most powerful when you combine it with what you know about your business.

- Launched a new Instagram campaign last month? Check if social traffic went up.
- Got featured on a podcast? Look for a spike in referral or direct traffic.
- Changed your homepage headline? See if your bounce rate improved.
- Raised your prices? Check if conversion rates shifted.

### Ask Questions

If something in your report surprises you, good or bad, ask us about it. We're here to help you understand what the data means and what (if anything) you should do about it.

Some good questions to ask:

- "Why did my traffic drop this month?"
- "Which blog post is driving the most traffic?"
- "Are people actually reading my retreat page or just glancing at it?"
- "What can I do to improve my conversion rate?"

## What a Good Report Looks Like

Every business is different, but here are some general benchmarks for wellness service businesses.

| Metric | Healthy Range | Great |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly visitors | 200-1,000 | 1,000+ |
| Pages per visit | 2-3 | 4+ |
| Average session duration | 1-2 minutes | 3+ minutes |
| Bounce rate | 40-60% | Under 40% |
| Conversion rate | 1-3% | 3-5%+ |

These are rough guidelines, not hard rules. A retreat center with 300 highly targeted visitors converting at 5% is doing better than a coaching site with 5,000 visitors converting at 0.5%.

## Taking Action on Your Report

The best report in the world is useless if you don't do anything with it. Here are some practical next steps based on common report findings.

**Traffic is growing but conversions aren't?**
Your call-to-action might need work. We can help you test different button text, placement, or messaging.

**Most traffic comes from one source?**
That's a risk. If Google changes its algorithm or your Instagram account gets restricted, you'd lose most of your traffic. Consider diversifying.

**A specific page has high traffic but high bounce rate?**
The page might not be delivering on what people expected. We can review the content and suggest improvements.

**Traffic dropped suddenly?**
Don't panic. Check if there's a seasonal pattern or if something changed (new website, different URL structure). We'll investigate and let you know.
