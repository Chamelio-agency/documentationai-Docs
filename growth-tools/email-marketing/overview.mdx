# Email Marketing Overview

Here's something that might surprise you: email is still the single most effective marketing channel for small businesses. Not Instagram, not TikTok, not Facebook. Email.

The reason is simple -- you own your email list. No algorithm decides who sees your message. When you send an email, it goes straight to your subscriber's inbox. That's a direct line to people who already told you they want to hear from you.

---

## Why Email Matters for Wellness Businesses

If you run a retreat, a coaching practice, or a coliving space, your business is built on relationships. People don't book a week-long retreat on impulse. They follow you, read your content, build trust with you over time, and then -- when the moment is right -- they book.

Email is the perfect tool for that journey. It's personal, direct, and intimate. Unlike a social media post that disappears in 24 hours, an email sits in someone's inbox until they're ready to read it. And when they do, it feels like a one-on-one conversation, not a broadcast.

Think about it from your client's perspective: they found your retreat on Google, loved what they saw, but aren't ready to commit yet. They sign up for your newsletter. Over the next few weeks, they receive a warm welcome email, a story about why you started your business, a testimonial from a past guest, and finally, an early-bird announcement for your next retreat. By the time they book, they already feel like they know you.

That's the power of email.

## What You Can Do with Email Marketing

Your email marketing setup gives you everything you need to stay in touch with your audience. Here's what's available to you:

### One-Time Campaigns

These are emails you send once to your list (or a specific group on your list). Perfect for:

- **Retreat announcements** -- "Our spring retreat in Portugal is now open for booking"
- **Promotions and offers** -- "Early-bird pricing ends Friday"
- **Important updates** -- "We've moved to a new location" or "New coaching packages available"
- **Event invitations** -- "Join our free online breathwork session this Saturday"

You write it, pick who receives it, and send. One and done.

### Automated Email Sequences

These are series of emails that go out automatically based on a trigger. Once set up, they run on autopilot. Common examples:

- **Welcome sequence** -- When someone signs up for your newsletter, they automatically receive a series of 3-5 emails over the next couple of weeks introducing your business, sharing your story, and highlighting your services.
- **Post-booking sequence** -- After someone books a retreat or session, they receive preparation emails, logistics, what to expect, and a "we can't wait to meet you" message.
- **Follow-up sequence** -- After a retreat or session ends, clients receive a thank-you email, a feedback request, and an invitation to book again.

The beauty of automated sequences is that they work while you sleep. Someone signs up at 2 AM on a Sunday? They get their welcome email immediately.

### Newsletters

Regular updates you send to your audience to stay top of mind. This could be:

- Monthly updates about your business
- Wellness tips and insights
- Behind-the-scenes stories
- Upcoming events and retreats
- Blog post highlights

Newsletters don't need to be fancy. A short, heartfelt update every couple of weeks is enough to keep your audience engaged and thinking about you.

## How It Works

Your email marketing is built right into your Growth Tools dashboard. Here's how the pieces fit together:

**Your contacts are already there.** Everyone who fills out a form on your website, books a call, or is added to your CRM is a potential email recipient. No need to export lists or juggle multiple tools -- it's all in one place.

**You send from your own email address.** Emails go out from something like hello@yourbusiness.com -- not from a generic no-reply address. This builds trust with your audience and significantly improves deliverability (the chances of your email actually landing in someone's inbox instead of spam).

**Everything syncs with your CRM.** When someone opens your email, clicks a link, or unsubscribes, that information is automatically recorded on their contact profile. You can see exactly who's engaged and who's gone quiet.

**Design is drag-and-drop.** You don't need to know any code to create beautiful emails. The email editor lets you drag in text blocks, images, buttons, and dividers. There are also templates to get you started quickly.

## Types of Emails at a Glance

Here's a quick overview of the three types of emails you can send:

| Type | When to use it | How it works |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| **Campaign** | One-time announcements, promotions, invitations | You create it, choose your audience, and send or schedule it |
| **Automated Sequence** | Welcome series, post-booking follow-ups, nurture sequences | Set it up once, and it runs automatically every time someone triggers it |
| **Newsletter** | Regular updates, tips, stories, blog highlights | You create and send it on a recurring schedule (weekly, biweekly, monthly) |

## A Note on Email Best Practices

Before you start sending, a few guiding principles to keep in mind:

**Only email people who want to hear from you.** This is both a legal requirement (GDPR) and good practice. Every person on your list should have opted in -- either by filling out a form, booking a service, or explicitly saying "yes, email me."

**Consistency beats volume.** One thoughtful email every two weeks will do more for your business than five rushed emails in one week. Your subscribers don't want to be bombarded.

**Make it personal.** Write like you're talking to one person, not an audience. Use their first name. Share real stories. Be yourself. That's what makes email so powerful for wellness businesses -- it feels human.

**Always preview on mobile.** More than half of all emails are opened on a phone. If your email looks great on desktop but terrible on mobile, you're losing half your audience.
