# Your Payments Dashboard

The payments dashboard is where you see everything related to money in your business — who's paid, what they bought, which subscriptions are active, and your full transaction history. Think of it as your financial command centre.

## Orders

### Managing a subscription

From the subscription detail page, you have several actions available. [Watch this step (2:10)](https://www.loom.com/share/2cb2deb6167f4724a773a38fa91f8e8c?t=130)

<Image
  src="https://loom.com/i/46980a2cbad64a898d5a4dcbf9e11649?workflows_screenshot=true"
  alt="Subscription actions menu showing options to pause, cancel, update payment, and more"
  width="800"
  height="450"
/>

Here's what each action does:

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="Pause a subscription" default-open="false">
    Temporarily stop billing without cancelling. Useful when a client is travelling or taking a break. You can resume it anytime and billing picks back up from where it left off.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Resume a subscription" default-open="false">
    Reactivate a paused subscription. The client will be billed again on the next cycle.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Cancel a subscription" default-open="false">
    Permanently stop the subscription. The client won't be billed again. If they want to restart later, you'd need to create a new subscription.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Share payment update link" default-open="false">
    If a client's card expired or they want to switch payment methods, you can send them a link to update their card details themselves — no need for you to handle their payment info.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Update trial period" default-open="false">
    Extend or adjust a client's trial period if they need more time before their first payment.
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>

<Callout kind="info">
  When a subscription payment fails (for example, an expired card), the system automatically retries the charge. You can configure how many times and how often it retries in your [Payment Settings](/growth-tools/payments/settings). This prevents you from losing clients over a simple card issue.
</Callout>

### Adding a subscription manually

You can also create a subscription for someone directly from their contact profile. [Watch this step (3:01)](https://www.loom.com/share/2cb2deb6167f4724a773a38fa91f8e8c?t=181)

<Image
  src="https://loom.com/i/e7b9661e9ffc41559b2ebf99398ecbde?workflows_screenshot=true"
  alt="The manual subscription creation form showing product selection and billing start date"
  width="800"
  height="450"
/>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the client's contact profile" icon="user" title-type="p">
    Find them in your contacts and open their profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a subscription" icon="plus-circle" title-type="p">
    Select the billing start date and choose the product they're subscribing to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add payment details" icon="credit-card" title-type="p">
    If you already have their card on file, you can select it. Otherwise, you'll need their card details — or send them a payment link instead, which is usually easier.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">
  In most cases, it's easier to send the client a payment link and let them enter their own card details. That way you never need to handle sensitive payment information yourself.
</Callout>

## Transactions

The Transactions page is your complete money history — every single payment, refund, and failed charge. [Watch this step (3:36)](https://www.loom.com/share/2cb2deb6167f4724a773a38fa91f8e8c?t=216)

<Image
  src="https://loom.com/i/6ad3d1f9e9064bf8b5aee949a4638f8e?workflows_screenshot=true"
  alt="The Transactions page showing a list of all payments with status, amount, and date"
  width="800"
  height="450"
/>

Each transaction shows the date, contact name, amount, status (succeeded, failed, refunded), and where the payment came from.

### Issuing a refund

If you need to refund a client, this is where you do it. Find the transaction and click **Refund**. The money goes back to the client's original payment method.

<Callout kind="alert">
  Double-check the transaction details before processing a refund — once issued, it can't be reversed. It typically takes 5-10 business days for the refund to appear in the client's account.
</Callout>

## Quick reference

| What you want to do | Where to find it |
| --- | --- |
| See all purchases | **Payments → Orders** |
| Track recurring payments | **Payments → Subscriptions** |
| View full payment history | **Payments → Transactions** |
| Refund a client | **Transactions → find transaction → Refund** |
| Pause a subscription | **Subscriptions → click subscription → Pause** |
| Export for your accountant | **Download button** (on Orders or Transactions page) |
| Send payment update link | **Subscriptions → click subscription → Share payment update** |
