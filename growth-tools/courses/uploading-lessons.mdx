# Uploading Lesson Videos & Captions

Once your [course structure is set up](/growth-tools/courses/creating-courses), you'll want to add video content to your lessons. This quick guide walks you through uploading a video, choosing a thumbnail, and adding captions.

## Uploading a video

Open the lesson you want to add a video to and click the video upload area. [Watch this step (0:00)](https://www.loom.com/share/7a3198845af546c99c987f682a454016?t=0)

<Image
  src="https://loom.com/i/9d5c586a7dc149aea356e48539a8dbc3?workflows_screenshot=true"
  alt="The lesson editor showing the video upload area"
  width="800"
  height="450"
/>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click to add a video" icon="upload" title-type="p">
    Click the video upload area and select your video file from your computer. [Watch this step (0:11)](https://www.loom.com/share/7a3198845af546c99c987f682a454016?t=11)

   <Image
     src="https://loom.com/i/bdf4123dcae948dc8029bd90134d43d1?workflows_screenshot=true"
     alt="Selecting a video file to upload to the lesson"
     width="800"
     height="450"
   />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for processing" icon="clock" title-type="p">
    The video needs to upload and then get transcribed by the system. This can take a few minutes depending on the file size — be patient and don't navigate away from the page while it's processing. [Watch this step (0:22)](https://www.loom.com/share/7a3198845af546c99c987f682a454016?t=22)

   <Image
     src="https://loom.com/i/3667c50c43fc43f08bd4d0e91daedc81?workflows_screenshot=true"
     alt="Video uploading and being transcribed — progress indicator visible"
     width="800"
     height="450"
   />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a thumbnail" icon="image" title-type="p">
    Once the video is fully uploaded and transcribed, you can select a thumbnail — this is the preview image clients see before they hit play. You have two options:

   - **Use a video frame** — play the video to the moment you want, pause, and click "Use current frame as thumbnail"
   - **Upload a custom image** — upload your own designed thumbnail (recommended size: 1280x720 or larger, 16:9 ratio)

   [Watch this step (2:00)](https://www.loom.com/share/7a3198845af546c99c987f682a454016?t=120)

   <Image
     src="https://loom.com/i/bb9e347c465e43a48cac6a2bb1408c0a?workflows_screenshot=true"
     alt="Thumbnail selection showing available frames from the uploaded video"
     width="800"
     height="450"
   />
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Keep your video files as small as practical. Shorter, focused lessons (5-15 minutes) tend to perform better than long recordings. If you have a 60-minute session, consider splitting it into 4-5 shorter lessons.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="info">
  The thumbnail you select applies to both the **lesson tile** (what clients see in the course overview) and the **video player** (the image shown before they hit play). If you want different images for each, you can set the video frame as the player thumbnail and upload a separate custom image as the lesson thumbnail.
</Callout>

You can also import videos you've already uploaded to your media storage instead of re-uploading them. This saves time if you use the same video across multiple lessons or courses.

## Adding captions

Captions make your courses accessible to more people — including those who are hard of hearing, watching without sound, or speak a different language. [Watch this step (2:19)](https://www.loom.com/share/7a3198845af546c99c987f682a454016?t=139)

<Image
  src="https://loom.com/i/61be48cc2e5e415580bf1a47e2240e44?workflows_screenshot=true"
  alt="The captions section showing language selection for uploading caption files"
  width="800"
  height="450"
/>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the captions section" icon="subtitles" title-type="p">
    Below the video, you'll find the captions area. Select the language for your captions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate captions using an external tool" icon="zap" title-type="p">
    The system doesn't auto-generate captions yet, so you'll need to use a third-party tool to create them. Upload your video to one of these services and download the caption file (only **.srt format** is supported):

   - **YouTube** — upload your video as unlisted, wait for auto-captions, download the subtitle file
   - **Descript** — transcribes and lets you edit captions
   - **Otter.ai** — fast transcription with export options
   - **Rev** — professional captions if you want higher accuracy

   [Watch this step (2:28)](https://www.loom.com/share/7a3198845af546c99c987f682a454016?t=148)

   <Image
     src="https://loom.com/i/0bafb94d329e48b4a45c626a8a699b68?workflows_screenshot=true"
     alt="Uploading a caption file to the lesson platform"
     width="800"
     height="450"
   />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the caption file" icon="upload" title-type="p">
    Upload the .srt file. It will sync with your video based on the timestamps in the file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and fine-tune" icon="check" title-type="p">
    After uploading, you can use the **built-in subtitle editor** to fix any errors. The editor shows each caption line with its timestamp — click any line to jump to that moment in the video. If you spot overlapping lines or timing issues, warning icons will appear so you can fix them. There's also a **Raw Mode** for manual editing if you prefer working with the .srt format directly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="info">
  You can add captions in multiple languages by repeating the process for each language. This is great if you have international clients — for example, adding both English and Dutch captions for your Belgian and Netherlands audience.
</Callout>

<Callout kind="tip">
  The quickest free method: upload your video to YouTube as an unlisted video, wait for the auto-generated captions (usually takes a few minutes), then download the subtitle file from YouTube Studio and upload it here. The quality is surprisingly good for most spoken content.
</Callout>

## Quick reference

| What you want to do | How |
| --- | --- |
| Upload a lesson video | Open lesson → click video area → select file |
| Set a thumbnail | After upload completes → choose a frame |
| Add captions | Captions section → select language → upload .srt/.vtt file |
| Add captions in another language | Repeat the caption upload for each language |
